What is PDA (Patent Ductus Arteriosus)?

Details

Patent Ductus Arteriosus (PDA) is one of the most common congenital heart defects — a heart problem that is present from the moment a baby is born. The word “patent” simply means “open,” and that is exactly what the condition is: a tiny blood vessel that is supposed to close after birth instead stays open.

Before a baby is born, a small vessel called the ductus arteriosus connects the two main arteries leaving the heart — the pulmonary artery and the aorta. In the womb a baby’s lungs aren’t yet used for breathing, so this vessel lets blood bypass them. It is a normal, essential part of every baby’s circulation before birth.

Once a newborn takes its first breaths, the ductus arteriosus is meant to close on its own within the first days or weeks of life. In a child with PDA, it never fully closes. Blood that should flow out to the body instead leaks back toward the lungs and recirculates — forcing the heart and lungs to work far harder than they should, around the clock.

How the heart is affected

  • The ductus arteriosus is meant to close shortly after birth.
  • When it stays open, oxygen-rich blood flows back into the lungs, instead of the body.
  • The heart is forced to work 30-50% harder to compensate.

Why PDA Matters

If left untreated, PDA can lead to:

  • Heart failure
  • Pulmonary hypertension
  • Poor growth & development
  • Permanent lung damage

PDA is responsible for 5-8% of all congenital heart diseases.
That’s approximately 20,000+ new cases each year in India alone.

The Good News

  • PDA can be treated with a simple, 1-2 hour procedure.
  • A tiny stent or device is inserted via catheter, with no open-heart surgery needed.
  • The surgery has a 98%+ success rate and a quick recovery time.
  • Cost: around ₹1,00,000, a barrier for many families.
